Arrange your jury service

Fill in the online form to arrange your jury service.

  • You’ll need your juror number from the letter we sent to you
  • You must arrange your service within 7 days of receiving your letter
  • You’ll be able to apply to delay the date or be excused if you have a genuine reason for doing so
  • Arranging your jury service will take less than 10 minutes

It’s a criminal offence punishable by a fine of up to £1,000 if you:

  • refuse to provide information to determine whether you’re qualified to be a juror
  • give false information in order to get out of doing jury service, or you allow someone to do this on your behalf

Start now

How long will it last?

Jury service usually lasts for up to two weeks. If a trial is likely to last longer you’ll be asked if this will be difficult for you. You may sit on more than one trial.

How do I take time off work?

Show your employer the letter we’ve sent you, to confirm your jury service. They must let you take the time off work but might ask you to rearrange the date.

What can I claim?

You should be able to claim for travel, food and drink, loss of earnings and other expenses.

What will I do on jury service?

On your first day at court, you’ll be shown a film called 'Your role as a juror'. It explains what happens in the courtroom and your responsibilities.
